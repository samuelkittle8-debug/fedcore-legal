FedCore Pay

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025  ·  Last Updated: January 1, 2025
Summary: FedCore Pay is a payment operations platform. We collect only the information necessary to process payments, verify bank accounts, and comply with financial regulations. We do not sell your data.

1. Who We Are

FedCore Pay ("FedCore," "we," "us," or "our") is a payment processing platform that enables businesses to send and receive ACH transfers, verify external bank accounts, and manage payment operations. Our services are provided to verified business customers and their authorized users.

For questions about this policy, contact us at: privacy@fedcorepay.io

2. Information We Collect

Account and Identity Information

  • Full legal name and business name
  • Email address and phone number
  • Business address and tax identification number (EIN)
  • Government-issued identification for authorized users
  • Date of birth (for identity verification as required by law)

Banking and Financial Information

  • Bank account numbers and routing numbers (stored encrypted using AES-256)
  • Account type (checking or savings)
  • Account holder name
  • Micro-deposit verification amounts (deleted immediately after verification)
  • Transaction history related to payments processed through our platform

Payment Transaction Data

  • Payment amounts, dates, and reference numbers
  • Recipient names, routing numbers, and account identifiers
  • Payment status, settlement dates, and ACH trace numbers
  • Memo fields and payment descriptions

Technical and Usage Information

  • IP addresses and device information
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Login timestamps and session activity
  • API access logs

3. How We Use Your Information

  • Processing payments — To initiate, route, settle, and reconcile ACH transfers on your behalf
  • Account verification — To verify external bank accounts through micro-deposit trial deposits
  • Identity verification — To comply with Know Your Customer (KYC) and Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) requirements
  • Fraud prevention — To detect and prevent unauthorized transactions, money laundering, and financial crime
  • Regulatory compliance — To comply with NACHA operating rules, FinCEN requirements, and applicable federal and state laws
  • Customer support — To respond to inquiries, disputes, and ACH return items
  • Security — To protect the integrity of our platform and your accounts
  • Service improvement — To analyze platform usage and improve our payment infrastructure

4. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We share data only in the following circumstances:

  • Payment processors — We use Stripe, Inc. to process payments. Bank account information is shared with Stripe to initiate and settle ACH transfers. Stripe's privacy policy is available at stripe.com/privacy.
  • Banking partners — Account information is shared with originating and receiving depository financial institutions (ODFIs and RDFIs) as required to execute ACH transactions through the NACHA network.
  • Regulatory authorities — We may disclose information to the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN), NACHA, federal or state banking regulators, or law enforcement when required by law or legal process.
  • Within your organization — Users within your business account can view transaction data based on their assigned role and permissions.
  • Business transfers — In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

5. Bank Account Data Security

We treat banking information as our most sensitive data. All bank account numbers are encrypted at rest using AES-256-CBC encryption. Routing numbers and account last-four digits are retained for operational and audit purposes. Full account numbers are decrypted only at the moment of payment submission and are never logged or stored in plaintext anywhere in our systems.

Micro-deposit amounts used for trial verification are encrypted during the verification window and permanently deleted upon successful verification.

6. Data Retention

We retain payment transaction records and associated account information for a minimum of 7 years from the date of the transaction, as required by the Bank Secrecy Act and NACHA rules. Verified bank account records are retained for the duration of your account plus 7 years following account closure.

You may request deletion of non-regulated personal data by contacting us at privacy@fedcorepay.io. We will honor deletion requests to the extent permitted by our legal and regulatory retention obligations.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access — Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Correction — Request correction of inaccurate personal data
  • Deletion — Request deletion of data not subject to legal retention requirements
  • Portability — Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Objection — Object to certain types of processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@fedcorepay.io. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Cookies and Tracking

Our platform uses session cookies strictly necessary for authentication and security. We do not use advertising cookies, cross-site tracking, or third-party analytics services that share data with advertisers. You may disable cookies in your browser settings; however, doing so will prevent you from logging in to the platform.

9. Children's Privacy

FedCore Pay is a business-to-business platform intended solely for use by adults operating registered business entities. We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under the age of 18. If you believe a minor has provided us information, contact us immediately at privacy@fedcorepay.io.

10. International Data Transfers

FedCore Pay operates in the United States and processes all payment data on servers located in the United States. If you access our platform from outside the US, your information will be transferred to and processed in the United States, where data protection laws may differ from those in your jurisdiction.

11. Third-Party Services

Our platform integrates with the following third-party services:

  • Stripe, Inc. — Payment processing and bank account verification (stripe.com/privacy)
  • NACHA — ACH network operator governing electronic funds transfers

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page and notify active account holders by email at least 14 days before the changes take effect. Continued use of the platform after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
